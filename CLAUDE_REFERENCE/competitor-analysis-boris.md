# BORIS Software — Competitor Analysis

> Executive Summary: What BORIS is, strengths, weaknesses, and how we beat them.

---

## Executive Summary

**What BORIS is:** A UK-based all-in-one field service and construction management platform, providing modules for job scheduling, workforce/asset tracking, compliance forms, and more. It digitizes site operations with mobile apps and cloud data to replace paperwork.

**Top Strengths:**
- **Comprehensive Feature Set:** Covers CRM, scheduling, live GPS tracking, asset & stock management, HSE compliance, reporting – a true end-to-end solution.
- **Field Visibility:** Excellent real-time visibility of field teams via map GPS tracking, geofencing, and even live markup of floor plans for collaboration.
- **Customizable Forms & Compliance:** Highly flexible mobile forms (with photos, signatures, NFC/barcodes) to capture data on-site for audits, safety, and quality control.

**Top Weaknesses:**
- **Complexity for Small Users:** Steep learning curve and initial setup effort – small teams found BORIS Lite confusing/buggy, with some promised features not working. It can feel overwhelming due to its breadth.
- **Performance & UX Issues:** In past versions, the interface was outdated (improved in 2023). Large projects or reports can be slow to load on both mobile and desktop. Occasional app crashes have been reported.
- **Integration & Cost Barriers:** Lacks turn-key integrations (accounting, etc.) – requires custom solutions. Full functionality is locked behind costly "Professional" plans (~£3k/year), making it expensive for some mid-size firms.

**Biggest Opportunity:** Beat BORIS on simplicity and usability. A modern alternative can win by offering an intuitive UI, quick onboarding (no heavy training or paid "virtual assistant" needed), and built-in automation/integrations. By focusing on the core workflows (quotes → jobs → invoices) with less clutter, plus affordable pricing for small/medium contractors, a new product can capture users who find BORIS too complex or pricey.

## What BORIS Is (Product Identity)

- **Category:** Field Service Management (FSM) and Construction Operations software (cloud-based SaaS). Positioned as an all-in-one job management and compliance platform.
- **Target Customers:** Scalable from small contractors to enterprise – claims to serve 5-person teams up to large enterprises (even NHS facilities). Sweet spot is mid-sized construction, trades, and maintenance companies (dozens to a few hundred employees) that need robust compliance tracking.
- **Industries Served:** Primarily construction and related trades (fire protection, civil engineering, groundworks, HVAC, facilities maintenance). Also used in utilities, healthcare maintenance, pest control, and other field services where safety and asset tracking are critical.
- **Core Problem Solved:** Eliminating paperwork chaos and coordinating field operations. BORIS streamlines job planning, workforce coordination, compliance checks, and record-keeping. The promise is to "put your business in the palm of your hand," giving managers real-time oversight and workers easy mobile tools.

## User Journeys

### 1. Lead → Quote → Job → Invoice → Follow-up
A sales or admin user enters a new lead into BORIS's CRM. They use the Pricing/Quoting module to generate a quote (pricing engine auto-calculates costs). Once accepted, the quote converts to a job. The scheduler assigns date, time, and field team in the Planner calendar. Field team is notified via app. After completion, an invoice is prepared – BORIS records job cost details which can be compiled into an invoice PDF or exported to accounting software (no native Xero/QB integration). Follow-up tasks can be scheduled, CRM marks opportunity as won.

**Gaps:** No automated way to send invoices or collect payments – users must use external systems. Follow-up relies on users setting tasks; no built-in marketing follow-up tools.

### 2. Field Technician Workflow
Tech opens BORIS mobile app → sees assigned jobs → gets all details (location with one-tap directions, job description, documents, required forms) → travels to site (GPS route logged) → uses Live Drawing to mark up floor plans → fills out checklist forms → captures photos (before/after) → gets client's digital signature → marks job done → syncs to cloud (or waits for signal) → logs time spent and materials used.

**Pain points:** Initial training (learning to navigate all forms) and occasional app slowness with large forms or many photos.

### 3. Office/Admin Workflow
Ops manager reviews Dashboard → uses Planner to schedule/adjust assignments → monitors job statuses in real time → watches map for field teams → handles inbound requests → generates reports (PDF with photos, signatures) → approves timesheets and expenses.

**Challenges:** Sheer amount of functionality – admins need to configure templates and dashboards. BORIS offers a paid "Virtual Assistant" service to help setup, indicating configuration can be labor-intensive.

### 4. Customer Experience
Client receives professional quote PDF → may get Client Portal access to track progress live → can sign off digitally on technician's device → immediately receives branded completion report (PDF with all evidence) → portal can show upcoming scheduled visits.

**Caveat:** Smaller companies using Lite might not enable portal. Some reports with many photos load slowly.

## What BORIS Does Really Well (Moat)

1. **All-in-one integrated structure** — Combines what many companies need 3-4 different tools to do. Once data is in BORIS, switching is hard because everything is interconnected.

2. **Real-time field visibility** — GPS tracking, mapping with live traffic, geo-fences. Few competitors have such integrated mapping. Live Drawing feature for collaborative floor plan markups is a standout.

3. **Compliance and audit trail** — End-to-end traceability: every form submission is time/GPS-stamped, documents version-controlled, detailed evidence reports at the push of a button. Built with high compliance industries in mind (fire safety, NHS).

4. **Flexibility/customization** — Described as "fully configurable to different industries and workflows." Companies can tailor form templates, KPIs, and have bespoke modules built.

5. **Offline-first mobile app** — Full offline functionality critical for remote construction sites. Rich media capture (photos, videos, training content).

## What BORIS Does Poorly (Pain Points)

### 1. Usability and Onboarding
- Steep learning curve (~1 month to appreciate benefits)
- Interface can feel cluttered with features and settings
- Smaller companies found it overwhelming to configure alone
- BORIS offers paid "Virtual Assistant" service for setup — indicates cumbersome configuration
- One 1-star review: "We were promised the world. Most of the features promised were not forthcoming… The Boris Lite system does not work."

### 2. Stability and Performance
- Large volumes of data (hundreds of photos, very large form outputs) = slow or crash
- "Due to the size of the reports… Boris software on both mobile and desktop can be slow to load"
- Healthcare user reported app "keeps crashing"
- Older UI was poor until 2023 refresh

### 3. Customer Support Inconsistency
- Generally praised by mid-size users
- Smaller "Lite" clients may get less hand-holding
- One user: "poor customer service. Salesman who promised several (unavailable) features refused to call… email ignored when asked for refund"

### 4. Integration and Data Export
- No native integration marketplace or open API
- Xero/QuickBooks connection requires custom solution
- No seamless calendar integration (aside from Outlook holiday sync for HR)
- Only standard outputs are Excel/PDF exports
- Competing FSM tools offer one-click integrations

### 5. Cost and Scalability for Small Users
- BORIS Lite at £29.99/user already significant for tiny companies
- Professional tier ~£3,000+/year — huge leap
- Lite version limited (only 1 "module" and fewer customization options)
- Can be overkill for very small teams

### 6. Mobile UI Quirks
- App not always self-explanatory — users needed "library of prompts" for tasks they don't do often
- Very long forms can be tedious on small screen
- Form fatigue without careful design

## What's Missing (Gaps & Opportunities)

1. **Streamlined Simplicity** — BORIS requires too much manual setup. Opportunity for intelligent defaults and automation (auto-assigning jobs based on location/skills, auto-sending client reminders, auto-generating follow-up jobs).

2. **Enhanced Communication** — Limited customer comms (mostly email of reports). Opportunity for SMS/WhatsApp updates, in-app chat/comments per job, "Uber-like" technician ETA notifications.

3. **Public API & Integrations** — Clear opportunity for well-documented API and pre-built integrations (Xero, QuickBooks, Sage, Zapier). "Works with your existing tools" as a major benefit.

4. **User-Friendly Customization** — More intuitive drag-and-drop form builder, template marketplace, setup wizard that auto-configures by industry.

5. **Modern UX** — Cleaner interface with role-based views. "Simple mode vs power-user mode" with progressive disclosure. Faster load times.

6. **Payment Collection** — Adding payment processing (Stripe) so customers can click "Pay Now" on digital invoices. BORIS doesn't handle this.

7. **AI and Predictive Insights** — Predictive maintenance suggestions, AI analysis of forms, digital scheduling assistant. BORIS's "Virtual Assistant" is human service, not AI.

8. **Focus on Specific User Roles** — Gantt charts for project managers, voice-driven app for field workers, deeper niche capabilities.

## Competitor Landscape

### Bolster Systems
- UK-based compliance management app, known for fire protection documentation
- Niche-focused — excels at recording inspections, defects, and remedial works
- Narrower than BORIS: great for compliance records but not full job scheduling/workforce management
- Companies in fire safety often evaluate BORIS vs Bolster
- **Opportunity:** Combine Bolster's ease in compliance with BORIS's wider functionality

### BigChange
- Prominent UK FSM platform (Leeds-based, since 2013, ~1,700 orgs)
- All-in-one like BORIS: CRM, scheduling/dispatch, live vehicle tracking, financial management, BI
- More geared to service businesses than heavy construction compliance
- Emphasizes being "easy to integrate" and user-friendly — something BORIS struggles with
- Premium-priced like BORIS
- **Opportunity:** Be as comprehensive but more affordable and modern in UI

### Jobber
- Popular North American FSM tool targeting small-medium home service businesses
- Offers quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and payment collection in a very user-friendly package
- Much simpler and more intuitive than BORIS, but far less feature-rich in compliance
- Built-in credit card processing (which BORIS lacks)
- Rated 4.6/5 vs BORIS's 4.4/5
- **Lesson:** Emulate Jobber's simplicity while offering some of BORIS's advanced features

### Commusoft
- UK-based FSM for plumbing, HVAC, facilities maintenance
- Stronger built-in accounting links (Xero, QuickBooks integrations)
- Better customer-facing tools (self-service booking portal, automated emails)
- BORIS has far superior compliance form capabilities and offline usage
- **Insight:** Combine BORIS's compliance strength with Commusoft's integration and customer-friendly features

### Simpro
- Global job management software (originating from Australia)
- Very powerful but complex (steep learning curve, pricey) — similar to BORIS
- Built-in accounting module and standardized workflows for recurring maintenance, purchasing
- Multi-company and contractor management features out-of-the-box
- **Lesson:** Provide robust project/job financial tracking but strive to be easier to use

### Connecteam
- Employee app rather than job management system
- Mobile app for schedules, time clock, forms, training, employee communication
- Highly user-friendly and affordable (4.6/5 from thousands of users)
- Lacks native customer/job management — internal-facing only
- **Opportunity:** Combine Connecteam's slick UX with BORIS's job management

### Other Notables
- **FieldPulse, Tradify, ServiceM8, Workever, JobLogic** — FSM platforms for small-mid businesses with quoting, scheduling, invoicing
- **Raken, Fieldwire** — Construction-project tools (daily reports, task management, plan markups)
- **Procore, Autodesk Construction Cloud** — Heavy-duty construction management suites for larger firms

## "Build a Better BORIS" Blueprint

### Product Principles

1. **Simplicity First** — Work out-of-the-box with minimal setup. 80/20 rule. Guide users through logical workflows instead of throwing dozens of options.

2. **Mobile-Centric Design** — If a feature doesn't work smoothly on a phone in a muddy construction site, it's not truly useful. Every workflow accessible via mobile and desktop.

3. **Modularity** — Core modules integrated, but let customers toggle what they need. Keep UI uncluttered for those who don't need certain features.

4. **Automation & Intelligence** — Infuse automation where BORIS requires manual intervention. Auto-scheduling, auto-reminders, auto-report generation.

5. **Integration-Friendly** — Open API, pre-built connectors. "Works with your existing tools" as a core promise.

6. **Customer-Centric** — Don't just manage internal operations. Make the customer experience (portal, communications, payments) a first-class feature.

## Summary

BORIS competes with pure-play FSM tools (Jobber, Commusoft) on one side and construction PM tools (Procore, Raken) on the other. Its unique selling point is blending field service ops with compliance-heavy features.

A "better BORIS" maintains this unique blend but delivers the usability of the Jobbers and Connecteams of the world, along with the open connectivity that modern SaaS customers expect.

**Our strategy:** Simplicity, integration, proactive automation, and a superior user experience — targeting the gaps BORIS left open.
